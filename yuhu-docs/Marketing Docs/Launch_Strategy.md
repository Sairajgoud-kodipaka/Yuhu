# 1. Introduction

This document outlines the complete **launch strategy** for Yuhu within a campus. It ensures maximum student adoption, smooth onboarding, strong visibility, and alignment with clubs and campus leaders.

Yuhu’s launch is executed in **3 Stages**:

1. Soft Launch (Department-Level)
2. Club Activation
3. Campus-Wide Launch

---

# 2. Launch Objectives

* Achieve 70–80% student installations in first department.
* Run fully online, secure elections.
* Activate all clubs on Yuhu.
* Establish Yuhu as the *only official communication channel*.
* Generate strong word-of-mouth and credibility.

---

# 3. Launch Strategy Summary

**The key strategy:**
Use elections as the **entry hook**, then convert the entire student body.

Because elections = high curiosity + everyone wants to see results.

---

# 4. Stage 1 — Department Soft Launch (Hook Stage)

## 4.1 Identify the First Department

* Preferably: B.S. / CSE (early adopters, tech-driven).

## 4.2 Launch Activities

* Posters announcing: "Vote on Yuhu"
* Classroom announcements
* WhatsApp teaser messages
* Personal outreach through class reps

## 4.3 Incentive Strategy

* Students install to vote.
* They stay for announcements.

## 4.4 Metrics for Stage 1

* 50% installs in 24 hours
* 200+ votes
* 1–2 verified announcements

---

# 5. Stage 2 — Club Activation (Trust Stage)

Once students install Yuhu for elections, use this momentum.

## 5.1 Bring Clubs Onboard

* Meet each club president.
* Create official club pages.
* Assign roles in app.

## 5.2 Publish 3–5 Club Announcements

Examples:

* Upcoming events
* Meeting reminders
* Member selection updates

## 5.3 Communicate Structure

Students realize:

* "If it’s on Yuhu — it’s official."

## 5.4 Metrics

* 5–7 clubs activated
* 10+ announcements in first week

---

# 6. Stage 3 — Campus-Wide Launch (Boom Stage)

Once clubs are active & elections succeed:

## 6.1 Announce "+ Yuhu is now our official communication partner"

Make it public through:

* Campus President
* Clubs
* Admin office endorsement (if possible)

## 6.2 Mass Promotion Activities

### Posters:

* Main entrance
* Cafeteria
* Library
* CSE block
* All club rooms

### Reels & Social Media:

* Testimonials from winners
* Election results screen
* "See all campus updates in one place"

### QR Codes Everywhere

* At events
* Classrooms
* Club pages

## 6.3 Push Notifications

* "Welcome to Yuhu — your official campus communication partner"
* "Follow your clubs to stay updated"

## 6.4 Metrics

* 60–80% campus installation
* 20+ daily active announcements in first month

---

# 7. Campus Partnerships & Communication

## 7.1 Campus President Role

* First public announcement
* Verifies all initial updates
* Enables legitimacy

## 7.2 Club Leaders

* Adopt Yuhu for all major announcements

## 7.3 Student Ambassadors (Optional)

* Promote in classrooms
* Answer student queries
* Manage event promotions

---

# 8. Offline + Online Marketing Stack

## 8.1 Offline

* Posters (QR codes)
* Class announcements
* Event booths
* Stickers / flyers

## 8.2 Online

* WhatsApp group blasts
* Instagram posts
* Reels showing voting/result screens

---

# 9. Event-Driven Growth Hacks

### Hack 1: Unlock Results Only in App

Students check results → installs spike.

### Hack 2: Club Competitions

"Most followed club wins a prize"

### Hack 3: Saturday Learning Sessions

Skill-sharing → strong engagement loop.

### Hack 4: Early Event Reminders

"First to know" push notifications encourage daily app opens.

---

# 10. Launch Scripts (Templates)

## 10.1 Classroom Script

"Hi everyone, our department election will be held on Yuhu.
Install the app using this QR code and verify your email.
You can vote only through Yuhu. Results will be live in the app."

## 10.2 Club Leader Script

"We have moved all our announcements to Yuhu. Follow our club page to stay updated. All events and information will be posted there first."

## 10.3 Campus President Script

"Yuhu is now our official campus communication partner. All verified announcements will be posted there. Please install it and follow your clubs."

---

# 11. KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)

* Install count
* Daily active users
* Announcement reach
* Club followers
* Votes cast
* Chat usage

Measuring KPIs helps refine rollout.

---

# 12. Risks & Mitigation

| Risk               | Mitigation                             |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| Student hesitation | Use elections as hook                  |
| Low club activity  | Provide templates and onboarding       |
| Spam               | Role-based messaging only              |
| Tech issues        | Pre-launch testing plus local fallback |

---

# 13. Summary

The Yuhu launch strategy is built on **momentum, verification, and community excitement**.
By starting with elections, onboarding clubs, and then expanding campus-wide, Yuhu achieves immediate trust and long-term engagement.
