# 1. Introduction

Yuhu is an official, open-source campus communication platform designed to bring structured, verified, and transparent communication to universities and colleges.

This proposal outlines how Yuhu can partner with your campus to improve communication, student engagement, club management, and digital governance.

---

# 2. Executive Summary

Campuses face increasing challenges in maintaining clear, reliable, and timely communication with students. WhatsApp groups and scattered platforms cause confusion, misinformation, and last‑minute announcements.

**Yuhu solves this by centralizing communication through a verified, campus‑endorsed system.**

With features like official announcements, event updates, digital elections, club spaces, communities, and learning sessions, Yuhu becomes the *single source of truth* for campus communication.

This partnership provides your institution with a modern, structured communication network that increases student engagement, reduces confusion, and strengthens administrative transparency.

# 3. What Yuhu Provides to the Campus

## 3.1 Official Communication System

* Verified announcements by campus leaders
* Department‑wise and campus‑wide distribution
* Push notifications ensuring students never miss important updates

## 3.2 Digital Elections Platform

* Transparent, fast, and secure voting
* Digital candidate listing
* One‑vote, one‑device enforcement
* Instant live results

## 3.3 Club & Department Management

* Dedicated digital pages for each club
* Club announcements and event management
* Committee‑level chat channels
* Role‑based access (President, VP, Committee, Students)

## 3.4 Event Marketing & Registrations

* Publish events with CTA buttons
* Reminders and countdown notifications
* Engagement analytics

## 3.5 Communities & Learning

* Weekly peer learning sessions
* Interest‑based channels (tech, design, etc.)
* Notes, roadmaps, and resource sharing

## 3.6 Open‑Source Collaboration

* Campus can contribute directly to Yuhu’s GitHub
* Tech clubs can maintain modules
* Students can build real features as contributors

# 5. Partnership Structure

## 5.1 Roles and Permissions

* **Campus President:** Highest communication authority
* **Faculty Advisors:** Optional oversight
* **Club Presidents:** Create and publish club updates
* **Committee:** Assist with announcements & community management
* **Students:** Receive official updates and participate in elections

# 7. Implementation Plan (14-Day Rollout)

### **Phase 1 — Setup (Days 1–3)**

* Create campus admin accounts
* Load departments, clubs, and leadership roles
* Configure branding (logo, colors)

### **Phase 2 — Pilot Run (Days 4–7)**

* Launch within one department
* Conduct digital elections
* Push first announcements

### **Phase 3 — Club Onboarding (Days 8–10)**

* All clubs join and set up pages
* Publish 2–3 events

### **Phase 4 — Campus Launch (Days 11–14)**

* Full rollout to all students
* Management announcement
* Students start following clubs and elections
