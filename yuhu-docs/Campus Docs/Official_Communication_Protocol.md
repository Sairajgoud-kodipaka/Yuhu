# 1. Introduction

This document defines the **official communication protocol** for all announcements, events, club messages, elections, and updates published through **Yuhu**.

The purpose of this protocol is to:

* Maintain transparency
* Prevent misinformation
* Create a structured communication flow
* Ensure every message is verified
* Establish accountability across roles

This protocol must be followed by **campus presidents, club leaders, committees, and authorized representatives** when using Yuhu.

---

# 2. Communication Principles

### ✔ Clarity

All announcements must be simple, direct, and free of ambiguity.

### ✔ Verification

Only predefined verified roles may publish campus-wide or club-wide announcements.

### ✔ Timeliness

Announcements should be published early enough to prevent last-minute confusion.

### ✔ Transparency

Every message shows who sent it, when, and under what role.

### ✔ Respect & Professionalism

No abusive, harmful, or misleading content is allowed.

---

# 3. Role-Based Communication Permissions

Yuhu uses a structured **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)** system.

## 3.1 Campus President / Management

Allowed to:

* Publish campus-wide official updates
* Approve or disapprove major club announcements
* Create elections
* Issue emergency notices
* Verify accounts of club leaders

## 3.2 Club Presidents

Allowed to:

* Publish club-level announcements
* Announce events
* Invite members
* Assign committee roles

## 3.3 Committee Members

Allowed to:

* Post internal committee updates
* Manage club chat spaces
* Assist in announcement drafts (admin approval required for major announcements)

## 3.4 Students

Allowed to:

* Receive announcements
* Participate in elections
* Follow clubs and join communities
* Engage with approved content

---

# 4. Announcement Categories

### 4.1 Campus-Wide Announcements

Examples:

* Holiday notices
* Exam updates
* Emergency alerts
* Centralized event announcements

**Posted by:** President / Management

### 4.2 Club Announcements

Examples:

* Event updates
* Meeting reminders
* Opening registration

**Posted by:** Club Presidents / Committee (if allowed)

### 4.3 Academic Updates

Examples:

* Seminar announcements
* Department-level notices

**Posted by:** Department heads or assigned academic admins

### 4.4 Elections Communication

Examples:

* Candidate lists
* Voting timelines
* Result announcements

**Posted by:** President / Election Committee

---

# 5. Communication Flow & Approval System

## 5.1 Draft → Review → Publish

Depending on role:

* **President:** Can publish instantly
* **Club Presidents:** Can publish instantly for club-level updates
* **Committee:** Must send draft to president/club leader for approval

## 5.2 Verified Badge

Any message from:

* Campus President
* Official club leaders
  will carry a **“Verified”** badge.

This badge indicates that the information is official and trusted.

---

# 6. Content Guidelines

## 6.1 Required Elements in Every Announcement

* Clear title
* Short description
* Relevant tags (club, event, academic)
* Optional: image, link, CTA

## 6.2 Format Guidelines

* Use simple language
* Avoid long paragraphs
* No slang or informal communication for official updates
* Avoid misleading or ambiguous messaging

## 6.3 Timing Standards

* Event announcements: **minimum 48 hours before event**
* Election announcements: **minimum 72 hours before voting**
* Emergency notices: immediately

---

# 7. Election Communication Protocol

Elections require stricter communication rules.

## 7.1 Election Setup

* Only the President/Election Committee can create elections
* Candidate details must be accurate and verified

## 7.2 During Elections

* No biased or political announcements
* No misinformation
* Yuhu will notify all eligible students

## 7.3 After Voting

* Results must be published transparently
* Yuhu auto-generates results to eliminate manipulation risks

---

# 8. Community & Chat Protocol

Community features are designed for engagement but must be moderated.

### Allowed:

* Learning discussions
* Event feedback
* Peer collaboration

### Not Allowed:

* Hate speech
* Harassment
* Spam or irrelevant promotions
* Sharing private or sensitive data

Committee/Admin roles can remove inappropriate messages or report misuse.

---

# 9. Accountability & Responsibility

Every message sent on Yuhu is attached to:

* Sender's name
* Role
* Timestamp

This ensures all communication can be traced back for accountability purposes.

---

# 10. Escalation Policies

If misinformation, abuse, or policy violations occur:

1. Committee flags content
2. Club president reviews
3. Campus admin takes final action
4. Offender may be restricted or removed from roles

Severe violations may lead to campus-level consequences.

---

# 11. Student Rights

Students have the right to:

* Receive correct and verified information
* Report misleading content
* Opt out of non-essential notifications
* Request correction of incorrect details in the system

Yuhu respects full transparency and student protection.

---

# 12. Summary

This protocol ensures that:

* Every update is legitimate
* Every role is accountable
* Every election is transparent
* Every club is structured
* Every student is informed

By following this communication protocol, campuses can establish a reliable and trustworthy communication ecosystem for all stakeholders.
